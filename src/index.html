Pomodoro

A timer based off the Pomodoro technique. You set how many rounds you want to complete and how long you want to work for, how long your breaks are and how long your extended time-out should be (the break for the final round).

There are buttons for pausing, skipping sessions, and stopping the timer. At the moment there aren't any audio cues.

The app was built with the help of p5.js and has a responsive design.

Tic Tac Toe

Picked up Greensock animations and applied them here to make a flashy tic tac toe game.

You play against the computer whose tactic is sometimes AI and other times pure luck.

The AI is a minimax algorithm. It analyses hypothetical moves and chooses the best move based on the endgame outcome. I've also implemented some form of depth, limiting the AI's foresight and therefore, creating a more realistic, human game experience.

NOTE:
X goes first. Scores are tallied by players (you and the PC, and not X and O)

Weather App

A weather app connected to the weather API at Wunderground.

The design is responsive, implements GSAP animations, and has a day and night theme.

To prevent bombarding the user with an overload of information, I made a navigation bar that sorted the data into 5 useful categories: temperature, weather, humidity, wind speed and precipitation. I also included a button to toggle between metric and imperial units.

Wiki Viewer

A Wikipedia search engine that connects to the Wikipedia API itself. You can search for your own articles or get a random list of articles. You can enter search with the Enter key.

The app gives you preview of the article and clicking on the article takes you to the full article on Wikipedia.

When present, the sidebar lists the results of your search and allows you to quickly navigate to the article preview.

Design is responsive and implements Greensock animations.
